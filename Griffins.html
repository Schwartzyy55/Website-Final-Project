GRAND RAPIDS GRIFFINS

The Grand Rapids Griffins are a professional hockey team in the American Hockey League (AHL) based in Grand Rapids, Michigan and play home games at Van Andel Arena.The franchise began in the now-defunct International Hockey League in 1996 and merged into the AHL in 2001. They are the AHL affiliate to the Detroit Red Wings of the National Hockey League, and are the 2013 and 2017 Calder Cup champions.

To be an NHL affiliate, you act as a farm team for NHL's Teams. This means that everyone on the Griffins and every other AHL team has the possibility to be called up to play in the NHL, as well as possibly making much more money.
